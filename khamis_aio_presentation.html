Section 02 · How it works

The All-In-One,
in three pieces.

Before walking through the scenarios, here's the mechanical foundation of the product. The AIO is structured as a 30-year HELOC that functions as your primary mortgage and your checking account simultaneously.

01
Daily interest calculation
Interest accrues each day on the current outstanding balance — not against a fixed amortization schedule. Lower balance today equals less interest tomorrow. This is what makes deposits effective immediately.
02
Deposits reduce principal
Paychecks land directly in the line. Every dollar that arrives is treated as a principal payment until it's spent. The longer money sits, the more interest you avoid. Spending pulls from the line as needed.
03
Full liquidity, always
Money you've paid in stays accessible — there's no separation between equity and savings. Need cash for an emergency or investment? Draw it back from the line. No new application, no underwriting.

Section 03 · Honest framing

Built-in safeguards,
and risks worth understanding.

No product is risk-free. But before walking through the legitimate risks, here's what's structurally working in your favor — features built into the AIO that make it genuinely safer for the disciplined borrower than a traditional mortgage.

What's working in your favor

Safeguard 01
20+ year track record
The All-In-One was launched by CMG Financial in 2005 and has been continuously offered for over two decades. The product was awarded a U.S. patent in 2009 and is currently available in all 50 states. It has operated through the 2008 financial crisis, the COVID rate cycle, and every major rate environment in between.
Safeguard 02
The line is your emergency fund
Unlike a traditional mortgage, every dollar you've paid in stays accessible. Job loss, medical event, market downturn — you draw from the line instead of missing payments or applying for new credit at the worst possible time. As demonstrated in the stress test, a $50K emergency costs under $1,000 in interest because your normal cash flow repays it.
Safeguard 03
First-lien HELOC structure
The AIO functions as a first-lien HELOC, not a second mortgage stacked on top of an existing loan. There's only one obligation, no payment shock from a separate amortizing first lien, and no requirement to refinance to access equity. Interest is calculated daily on the actual outstanding balance — never on a phantom amortization schedule.
Safeguard 04
Strict borrower screening
CMG only approves AIO borrowers with strong credit profiles, positive cash flow, and minimum FICO 700 (760+ for 10% down). This is a self-selecting population of disciplined, financially-stable borrowers — which is part of why historical performance has been favorable. You're being placed alongside borrowers with similar risk profiles, not subprime borrowers.

Risks worth understanding

Risk 01
Variable interest rate
The AIO is tied to the 1-month CMT plus margin, so the rate adjusts with the broader rate environment. A higher rate increases interest cost on the outstanding balance.
Why it's manageable: Your balance pays down so quickly that rate exposure shrinks rapidly. In Scenario 2, the AIO is gone in 17 months — long before rate cycles meaningfully impact total cost.
Risk 02
Discipline dependency
The product only works if surplus actually stays in the line. Lifestyle creep — increased spending, fewer deposits — directly erodes the projected payoff timeline.
Why it's manageable: Your verified bank statements show consistent surplus and stable spending. The discipline isn't aspirational — it's already your default behavior.
Risk 03
Higher initial rate
The AIO rate (7.652%) is roughly 1.4% higher than the comparison 30-year fixed (6.250%). On the surface, this looks like paying more.
Why it's manageable: The higher rate applies to a much smaller balance for a much shorter time. Total interest paid is $198K vs $803K — the rate gap is irrelevant in absolute dollars.
Risk 04
Income disruption
If a job is lost or income drops materially, the surplus shrinks and the payoff timeline extends. The minimum payment is interest-only, but missing it means accruing balance.
Why it's manageable: The line itself becomes your emergency fund. With $143K in starting reserves and an open credit line, you have liquidity buffers that a conventional borrower doesn't have access to.
Risk 05
Year 11 forced amortization
After year 10, the line begins amortizing at 1/240th per month, meaning required principal payments kick in. Some borrowers end up with payment shock.
Why it's manageable: In both scenarios, the AIO is fully paid off well before year 10 — by year 7 (Scenario 1) or year 1.4 (Scenario 2). Forced amortization never applies.

How it works
Five things that make it different
The mechanics are simple. The impact compounds every single month.
1
Your paycheck goes directly into the loan
Direct deposit hits the AIO account. Instantly, your principal balance drops by that amount. Interest begins calculating on a lower number from that day forward — before you've spent a single dollar.
2
Interest recalculates every night on your actual balance
A traditional mortgage calculates interest on the same fixed balance all month. The AIO looks at your real balance at the end of each day. Lower balance = less interest charged. This difference compounds into tens of thousands of dollars saved over the life of the loan.
3
Spend on a rewards credit card — maximize every dollar
Rather than spending directly from the AIO day-to-day, the optimal strategy is to put all purchases on a rewards credit card and pay the statement balance in full each month from the AIO. 4
Money saved is money earned
Every dollar you earn from work is taxed before you see it. But every dollar you save in mortgage interest comes to you completely tax-free. That makes the AIO's savings worth more, dollar-for-dollar, than equivalent earned income. When the loan saves you hundreds of thousands in interest, that's money your household keeps at full value.
5
Your equity is liquid — accessible anytime, structured to protect you
As you pay down principal, available credit opens back up in the line. Need funds? Draw directly from the line — no new loan, no appraisal, no closing costs, no tax on the withdrawal. Understanding the difference
How each loan actually works
Two mortgages can have the exact same rate and loan amount — and produce completely different outcomes. The difference comes down to one thing: how and when interest is calculated.
Part one
How a traditional mortgage amortizes
Amortization is the process of paying off a loan through fixed, scheduled payments over time. Here's exactly how it works — and why the early years look the way they do.
The mechanics
Fixed payment. Shifting split.
A traditional 30-year fixed mortgage has one payment amount that never changes. What does change — every single month — is how that payment is divided between interest and principal.

Interest is front-loaded by design. When a loan is structured over 30 years, the math of dividing a large balance into equal payments means that interest naturally makes up a greater share of each payment in the early years. As the balance decreases over time, so does the interest portion — and the principal portion grows in its place. This isn't unique to any lender or product; it's simply the mathematical result of how long-term fixed payment loans are structured. The earlier you are in the loan, the more of each payment is going toward interest — and the slower your equity builds as a result.

The crossover point
When principal finally takes the lead
On a 30-year fixed mortgage at a typical rate, you don't reach the point where more of your monthly payment goes to principal than to interest until roughly year 18 or 19. For the first two-thirds of the loan's life, interest dominates every payment.

This is simply how compound interest math works at scale. The structure is mathematically predictable — but it does mean that equity builds slowly in the early years, and total interest paid over 30 years can be substantial.
Equity access on a traditional loan
Equity builds — but it stays locked
As principal is paid down, equity accumulates in the home. However, that equity is not accessible without taking action — either a cash-out refinance or a separate HELOC, both of which require new applications, appraisals, and closing costs.

The fixed payment structure also means there's no built-in mechanism to accelerate payoff without making separate additional principal payments — which fewer than 4% of homeowners consistently do.
Part two
How the All In One Loan amortizes
The AIO doesn't follow a traditional amortization schedule. Interest is calculated on your actual daily balance — which means your cash flow directly determines how fast the loan pays down.
The core difference
Daily interest on a real, live balance
On the AIO, interest is not calculated on a fixed monthly balance. It is calculated every single day based on whatever your actual outstanding balance is at the end of that day.

Every deposit you make immediately lowers the balance that interest is being calculated on. Every dollar in the account is actively working to reduce your interest cost from the moment it arrives. Those daily interest charges accumulate throughout the month and are collected as a single payment on the 21st of the following month.
The daily interest formula

The compounding effect
Small daily reductions. Enormous long-term savings.
Notice what happened between Month 1 and Month 2 — the interest charge dropped by roughly $80 simply because the balance was lower every single day. That pattern repeats every month.

Lower balance → less interest charged → more cash flow reduces principal → balance drops faster → even less interest → repeat.

This self-reinforcing cycle is what allows borrowers with consistent cash flow to pay off a 30-year loan in a fraction of the time.
No fixed amortization schedule
Every month is different — entirely dependent on your average daily balance, shaped by your deposits and spending patterns.
Interest collected on the 21st
All daily interest charges are totaled and collected as a single payment on the 21st of the following month. Every dollar above that charge reduces your balance.
The credit limit structure
For the first 10 years, your full credit limit stays open. After year 10, it reduces by 1/240th per month — steady, predictable paydown while keeping equity accessible.
You control the pace
The more cash flow cycling through the AIO — and the longer it sits before expenses are paid via credit card — the faster the loan pays down. Behavior drives outcome.
The key distinction
A traditional mortgage calculates interest on a scheduled, predetermined balance on a fixed timetable. The AIO calculates interest on your actual, real-time balance every single day — meaning your financial behavior directly determines how much interest you pay and how quickly your loan is eliminated.
